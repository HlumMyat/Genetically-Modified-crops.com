What Are GM Crops?

GM crops are created when scientists insert a gene from one organism into a plant to give it a useful trait—like insect resistance or better growth in dry areas.

How Are They Made?

  1. Scientists find a useful gene from another species (like bacteria).
  2. The gene is copied and inserted into the plant’s DNA.
  3. The modified plant is grown and tested for safety and performance.

Examples of GM Crops

  • Bt Cotton – resists insect pests.
  • Golden Rice – contains extra vitamin A.
  • GM Corn – survives herbicide use and insect damage.
  • GM Soybeans – used in many processed foods./li>
